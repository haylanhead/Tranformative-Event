The History of The Computer and how they've changed over time

19th Century Computing

1801-1911

  • 1801 - the loom used wooden punch cards to automatically weave fabric designs, and it made making carpets and fabric designs much easier and faster than by hand.

  • 1821 - English mathematician Charles Babbage conceives of a steam-driven calculating machine that would be able to compute tables of numbers. Funded by the British government, the project, called the "Difference Engine" fails due to the lack of technology at the time.

  • 1853 - Swedish inventor Per Georg Scheutz and his son Edvard design the world's first printing calculator. The machine is significant for being the first to "compute tabular differences and print the results,"

  • 1890 - Herman Hollerith designs a punch-card system to help calculate the 1890 U.S. Census. The machine, saves the government several years of calculations, and the U.S. taxpayer approximately $5 million

  • 1911 - IBM is founded it took IBM 22 years to release an IBM branded product they started off by releasing electronic typewriters

Early 20th Century Computing

1931-1947

  • 1931 - The first actual computer was created, called the differential analyzer, it was the first large scale automatic general-purpose mechanical analog computer.

  • 1939 - Hewlett-Packard was founded and today you might know them as HP.

  • 1941 - the first digital computer was created and it was called the Atanasoff-Berry Computer it was the first of its kind to be able to store things on its main memory, it was also able to perform one operation every 15 seconds

  • 1947 - the first transistors are created and there is no more need for vacuums

Late 20th Century Computing

1953-1999

  • 1953 - Grace Hopper creates the first programming language called GOBOL which stands for Common, Business-Oriented Language.

  • 1968 - Intel is founded.

  • 1971 - the first microprocessor the intel 4004 was created putting computers leaps and bounds ahead of where they used to be.

  • 1972 - the first game console was created by a german-american called Ralph Baer.

  • 1973 - ethernet is created by Robert Metcalfe

  • 1976 - Apple is founded and they unveil the Apple I which featured a single circuit board and rom

  • 1977 - The Commodore Personal Electronic Transactor (PET) was released onto the market with a MOS Technology 8-bit 6502 microprocessor which controlled the screen keyboard and cassette player, additionally in 1977 the first West coast computer faire was held and during that event Apple released the Apple II which included a color screen and an audio cassette drive for storage.

  • 1981 - IBM’s first computer was released onto the market it featured the MS-DOS operating system Optional features include a printer, two diskette drives, extra memory, a game adapter and more.

  • 1983 - The Apple Lisa was made. It was the first personal computer to feature a GUI; it included Drop Down menus and Icons also in the year the Gavilan SC was released and it was considered to be the first personal computer to be sold with the term laptop.

  • 1984 - the first generation macintosh is announced from apple.

  • 1985 - Microsoft releases windows in response to the Apple lisa’s operating system

  • 1989 - Tim Berners-Lee, a British researcher at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN), submits his proposal for what would become the World Wide Web.

  • 1993 - The Pentium microprocessor advances the use of graphics and music on PCs.

  • 1996 - Sergey Brin and Larry Page develop the Google search engine at Stanford University

  • 1998 - the Ti 73 calculator was released and this was 140 times faster than the computers used to get the Apollo missions to the moon.

  • 1999 - WIFI standing for "wireless fidelity" is developed and it initially covered a distance of up to 300 ft.

21st Century Computing

2000-current day

  • 2003 - AMD releases their first 64 bit AMD Athlon processor it was able to run at a blistering speed of 1ghz - 3.2ghz.

  • 2006 - The first Apple MacBook pro hit the markets. It featured a two core intel cpu.

  • 2009 - Microsoft releases windows 7 an operating system that many people loved, with some people still using to this day even with its discontinued support

  • 2011 - The first Chrombook is released

  • 2016 - The first reprogrammable quantum computer was created that drastically improved the quantum pc technology because people could use one computer for more than 1 complex algorithm by reprogramming it with different algorithms.
